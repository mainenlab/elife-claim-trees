This claim registers the Lottem et al. (2016) result as the existing single-region additive-modulation finding that the present paper's brain-wide GLM result extends. The result is from the Mainen lab — the same group as the present paper — which is methodologically relevant: the optogenetic stimulation protocol (SERT-Cre + ChR2 in DRN) is the same as the protocol used here, so the additivity finding is not protocol-dependent across the two studies. What differs is the recording scope: Lottem et al. recorded in piriform cortex during odor stimulation; this paper records across 13 regions during a perceptual decision task.

The Lottem 2016 result has been in the literature since 2016, and the current paper's R1 reframing makes explicit what was previously implicit: the brain-wide finding here is not the first observation of additive 5-HT modulation, it is the first brain-wide demonstration. The R1 introduction (I1 per revision notes) frames this explicitly: "the only prior study to test this in a behaving animal outside visual cortex — Lottem et al. (2016), from our lab — found additive modulation in piriform cortex." The R1 discussion (D9) closes by noting that the brain-wide finding generalizes the single-region precedent.

This claim is moderate-epistemic as a literature interpretation; the underlying Lottem 2016 result is well-supported within its own paper, but the additive characterization there is implicit in the spontaneous-vs-evoked dissociation rather than tested with a GLM interaction term as in the present paper.
