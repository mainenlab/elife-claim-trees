This claim registers the prior receptor-specific demonstration of 5-HT-mediated gain control. Its role in the R1 graph is twofold. First, it gives the eliminative claim `rules-out-multiplicative-gain-control` a known and concretely-cited counter-case to engage with — the brain-wide additive finding does not contradict the visual-cortex gain-control finding directly, because the brain-wide signature is a population average over regions and could mask region-specific gain modulation in visual cortex. Second, it is one of the two anchors of the receptor-dependent reconciliation `reconciles-5ht1a-baseline-suppression-with-5ht2a-gain-control`: the same Azimi et al. (2020) paper that established the 5-HT2A gain component also dissociated a 5-HT1A baseline-suppression component, and the receptor dissociation is what allows the brain-wide additive mode and the visual-cortex gain mode to coexist as receptor-specific computational regimes.

The Azimi et al. (2020) result is itself moderate-epistemic from this paper's perspective — this paper does not reproduce it, and the receptor-pharmacological dissociation rests on agonist/antagonist tools with their own selectivity caveats. But the Azimi work is from a different lab, in a different region, with a different pharmacological methodology, and is widely cited; the literature-interpretation status here is appropriate.
