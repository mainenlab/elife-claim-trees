This prediction operationalises the perisomatic half of the compartmental-dissociation hypothesis as a falsifiable mechanistic claim about a magnitude manipulation. The dissociation would fail if doubling perisomatic inhibition collapsed dendritic spike rates, or if it reduced somatic firing without elevating AP threshold. Tested jointly by the firing-rate effect (`perisomatic-inhib-drops-firing-07hz`) and the dendritic-spike preservation result (`gamma-perisomatic-no-dendritic-spike-change`), which together establish the threshold-elevation mechanism with dendritic spikes spared.
