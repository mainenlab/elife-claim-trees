If the perisomatic compartment is the locus of AP-generation control, then perisomatic inhibition should not merely translate the I/O curve (a pure subtractive effect, characteristic of upstream input scaling) but should also compress its slope (a divisive effect, characteristic of conductance increase at the spike-initiation site). A purely subtractive response under perisomatic inhibition would suggest the compartment was acting as a passive input scaler rather than as the active gain-control site the hypothesis posits. Tested by `perisomatic-inhib-subtractive-divisive`, which characterises the I/O curve under doubled perisomatic inhibition.
