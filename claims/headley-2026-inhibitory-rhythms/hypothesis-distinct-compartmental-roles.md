The paper's framing in the abstract and introduction motivates the empirical work with this hypothesis: that the spatial targeting of inhibition (perisomatic vs distal dendritic) determines its computational role. The hypothesis is a dissociation claim — the two compartments are predicted to be functionally separable, not redundant. The dissociation is tested through a series of empirical contrasts: (1) firing rate effects of doubling each inhibition type (Figs 4–5), (2) preserved dendritic spike rates under perisomatic but not distal inhibition (Fig 5), and (3) selective gating of apical vs basal input streams (Fig 10). The hypothesis is older than this paper — it inherits from a substantial literature on PV vs SST function — but the paper sharpens it into a quantitative, mechanistic prediction in a single biophysical model.
