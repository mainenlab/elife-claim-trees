The U-shape is the diagnostic signature of feedback over spillover: spillover predicts that foveal decoding inherits from peripheral activity through receptive-field tails, which would yield a monotonic decay with eccentricity from the peripheral target outward (or from the fovea outward, if RF tails extend inward). Feedback to the foveal retinotopic representation, by contrast, predicts a return-to-foveal-peak — a U-shape across the eccentricity range. The control condition (direct foveal stimulation) is expected to show monotonic decay from the foveal center, providing a within-paper baseline against which the experimental U-shape is interpreted.
