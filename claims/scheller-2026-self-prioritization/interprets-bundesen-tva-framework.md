This claim registers the TVA framework as a discrete literature node because the paper's argument structure depends on it end-to-end. The central hypothesis `hypothesis-capacity-mechanism-not-weights` is stated in TVA terms: the question is whether self-association operates by changing the absolute C parameter or by redistributing the fixed w weights. The model-comparison claim `tva-capacity-model-wins` pits a single-C model against a condition-specific-C model — a comparison that only makes sense within the TVA decomposition. All downstream processing-rate empirical claims are measured in the TVA parameter space and their interpretation is conditional on TVA's structural commitments about what it means for attention to act at the level of encoding into aware short-term memory.

Registering TVA as a literature-context node makes explicit that the C-versus-w distinction is not an empirical finding of this paper — it is an inherited analytical framework that the paper deploys. If TVA's decomposition were wrong (if attentional selection doesn't factorize cleanly into C and w, or if the TOJ paradigm doesn't isolate the processing rate in the assumed way), the paper's headline interpretation that self-association "mobilises capacity rather than redistributing weights" would dissolve. The framework is the interpretive lens through which every processing-rate number becomes meaningful.

Epistemic status is moderate because TVA is a well-established and empirically well-supported framework for visual attention with decades of applications across paradigms, but alternative decompositions exist (e.g., single-stage bottleneck models, biased competition models, drift-diffusion accounts) and the C/w distinction is not universally accepted as the right carving of attentional selection.
