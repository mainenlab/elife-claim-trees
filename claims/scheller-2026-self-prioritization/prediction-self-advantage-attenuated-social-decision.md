  If the automatic perceptual self-prioritisation and the deliberate social-identity
  decoding stages compete for the same finite attentional resource, then requiring an
  explicit social decision (whose shape flickered first?) should attenuate, eliminate,

  proceed unimpeded, and the net measured advantage shrinks toward zero or flips toward
  an other-associated advantage.

This is the paper's most counterintuitive a-priori prediction and the strongest test of
the trade-off framing of the central hypothesis. A purely additive account in which
explicit and implicit self-prioritisation contributions stack would predict the opposite
— a larger self-advantage when participants explicitly attend to social identity. The
trade-off prediction here, by contrast, expects social-decoding context to consume the
resource that would otherwise drive the automatic perceptual prioritisation. Tested by
`self-prioritization-absent-social-decision` (Experiment 1: no self-advantage in social
decision; trend toward other-advantage) and confirmed more decisively by
`other-association-advantage-social-condition` (Experiment 2: −1.6 Hz other-advantage,
HDI excludes zero). The individual-differences signature of the same trade-off is
captured by `decisional-dimension-tradeoff`.
