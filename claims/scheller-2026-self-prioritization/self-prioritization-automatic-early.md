The automaticity claim is the paper's main theoretical contribution. The key evidence pattern is: self-advantage in perceptual decision (no social decoding required) + no advantage or reversal in social decision (explicit decoding required). This dissociation implies that the perceptual feature associated with the self is marked as salient without requiring the social identity to be decoded — i.e., the self-marker operates at or before the perceptual feature representation stage. The moderate epistemic status reflects that the dissociation is real but the "pre-conscious" and "early" interpretation requires accepting the authors' stage model, which is not directly tested.
