This is the central biological hypothesis of the paper. It commits to a specific
processing-stage account: self-association is not merely a late-decision bias (a
post-perceptual evaluation of "is this mine?"), but an early salience signal that biases
which features are selected into aware short-term memory in the first place. The
hypothesis is dissociable from a purely post-perceptual account by the prediction that an
attention-only task — requiring no explicit social judgement — should still reveal a
self-advantage. Confirmed (with the predicted decision-context reversal) by
`self-prioritization-perceptual-decision-automatic`, `self-prioritization-absent-social-decision`,
and `other-association-advantage-social-condition`, with `self-prioritization-automatic-early`
as the integrative interpretation.
