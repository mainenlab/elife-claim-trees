The hagstone neuropil is explicitly named in this paper — the name derives from its pierced, ring-like appearance (likened to a hagstone). It is a new named structure; the existence claim is grounded in clear serotonergic filling that matches the synapsin-defined boundary. TDC2, proctolin, and AstA also show immunoreactivity within this neuropil (Fig6C, D), but 5-HT is the most definitive marker. The epistemic status is strong because the synapsin-5-HT co-correspondence provides two independent markers for the same boundary.
