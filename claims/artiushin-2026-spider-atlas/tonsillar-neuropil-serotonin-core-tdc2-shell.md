This spatial compartmentalization within a single named neuropil — a core-shell transmitter organization — is a structurally meaningful claim distinct from the existence of the tonsillar neuropil itself (see tonsillar-neuropil-novel-structure). The explicit language "division of compartments" in the Results indicates the authors regard this as a substantive finding, not merely background description. It also establishes the tonsillar neuropil as a layered structure analogous in organizational logic to the arcuate body and PCB, which both show transmitter-specific sublayers.
