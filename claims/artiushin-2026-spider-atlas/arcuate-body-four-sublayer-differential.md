The four-sublayer claim refines and supersedes the existing arcuate-body-layers-neurotransmitter-distinguished claim, which referred only to "layers" generically. The key advance here is: (1) the explicit four-sublayer nomenclature with anatomical boundaries, and (2) the demonstration that each of nine transmitter/peptide systems innervates a distinct subset, with some filling only one sublayer (e.g., dopamine in posterior ABd only; AstA predominantly in antABv) and others spanning multiple (proctolin, FMRFamide). Columnar substructure is also observed within some layers (AstA puncta in posABv, Fig11G; flagstone patterns for TDC2, 5-HT in aABd).

This claim is the detailed empirical complement to the existing moderate-strength arcuate body claim; together they are the scaffold for the Discussion comparison with other spider species (C. salei, M. muscosa, T. spinipalpis, A. sclopetarius).
