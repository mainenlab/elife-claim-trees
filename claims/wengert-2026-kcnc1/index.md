## Abstract

This paper generates and characterizes the Kcnc1-A421V/+ heterozygous knock-in mouse model carrying the human KCNC1 A421V variant associated with epileptic encephalopathy. All knock-in mice die before 122 days. PV+ interneurons show selectively impaired potassium current density and maximal firing frequency, consistent with Kv3.1 loss-of-function partly attributable to impaired membrane trafficking. Inhibitory synaptic dysfunction is absent at juvenile stages (P16-21) but present by young adulthood (P32-42), indicating progressive deterioration. Excitatory neurons are not significantly affected at either age. RTN neurons and layer V PV-INs are impaired but more subtly than superficial cortical PV-INs, consistent with relative Kv3.2 compensation. In vivo two-photon imaging reveals hypersynchronous discharges in all mutant mice and increased excitatory cell activity during quiet rest. Continuous video-EEG confirms spontaneous convulsive seizures, myoclonic events, and SUDEP in 8/12 recorded mutants.
