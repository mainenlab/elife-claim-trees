## Abstract

This paper generates and characterizes the Kcnc1-A421V/+ heterozygous knock-in mouse model carrying the human KCNC1 A421V variant associated with epileptic encephalopathy. All knock-in mice die before 122 days. PV+ interneurons show selectively impaired potassium current density and maximal firing frequency, consistent with Kv3.1 loss-of-function. Inhibitory dysfunction is absent at juvenile stages (P16-21) but present by young adulthood (P32-42), indicating progressive deterioration. Excitatory neurons are not significantly affected.
