The insula-as-guilt-substrate hypothesis is the central theoretical bet of the paper. It synthesizes two prior literatures: (1) anterior insula activation in moral emotion paradigms, including the Yu & Koban guilt-signature work that the paper directly references for convergent validity, and (2) computational accounts of guilt as responsibility-contingent unhappiness about partner harm (Battigalli & Dufwenberg's psychological game theory; van der Bos et al.'s redress experiments). The paper's contribution is to operationalize guilt within a within-subject lottery paradigm where responsibility is varied by who-chose (Social vs Partner) while partner outcome is held constant, isolating the interaction term that the hypothesis predicts. The hypothesis is dissociable from the agency-as-aversion hypothesis (`agency-reduces-happiness`) by predicting an interaction with partner outcome rather than a main effect of agency.
