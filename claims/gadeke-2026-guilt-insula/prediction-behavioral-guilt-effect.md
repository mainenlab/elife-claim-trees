This prediction operationalises the behavioral signature that both the anatomical and computational guilt hypotheses must satisfy before any neural test is informative. It is logically prior to the insula and STS predictions: if happiness does not differ between Social and Partner conditions after negative partner outcomes, the neural contrasts have nothing to track and the entire research programme collapses. The prediction is intentionally agnostic about effect size and about own-outcome conditioning — those refinements (`guilt-effect-independent-of-own-outcome`) are independent specificity claims. Tested directly by `guilt-reduces-happiness-after-partner-loss` in both Study 1 (fMRI, N=40) and Study 2 (behavioural replication, N=44).
