The STS hypothesis extends the mentalizing literature (Frith & Frith; Saxe; Nicolle et al.) by conditioning STS recruitment on agency rather than on the mere presence of another's outcome. The paper distinguishes this from a generic "STS tracks others' outcomes" account by requiring the responsibility interaction: STS should track partner RPE in Social trials but not in Partner trials, even when partner RPE magnitudes are matched. This is testable only via the model-based fMRI design (GLM2) in which trial-by-trial partner RPEs computed from the responsibility model serve as parametric regressors. The hypothesis is structurally distinct from the insula hypothesis: insula encodes the affective consequence of guilt; STS encodes the cognitive operation of inferring the partner's experience of an outcome the participant caused.
