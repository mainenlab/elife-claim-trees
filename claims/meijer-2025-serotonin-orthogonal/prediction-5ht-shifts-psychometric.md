The prediction is what would have to be observed for the surprise-coding interpretation of 5-HT to survive in this task. The paper deliberately tests it across multiple operationalizations — fitting a four-parameter psychometric, comparing reaction times, fitting a probabilistic choice model with separate weights per stimulation condition (Fig. 3f-g) — to make the null result robust to choice of metric. None of these operationalizations show a significant difference. The prediction is refuted by `5ht-stim-leaves-decision-behavior-intact`. This refutation is the load-bearing empirical pivot of the paper: it is what motivates introducing the orthogonal-subspace hypothesis as the explanation for what the strong neural modulation is doing if it isn't shaping behavior.
