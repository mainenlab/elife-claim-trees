The quiet-wakefulness section of the paper is structured around this hypothesis. The three coordinated empirical observations — pupil dilation, ripple suppression, exploratory-behavior increase — each constitute a converging piece of evidence for a rapid offline→online state transition. The hypothesis itself is older than this paper (cf. Cazettes et al., Lottem et al., the broader literature on 5-HT and arousal state) but is operationalized here through the simultaneous tri-modal readout. None of the individual measures is decisive on its own — pupil could reflect light reflex, ripple suppression could reflect direct hippocampal effect, whisking could reflect a motor side effect — but the convergence across modalities at consistent latencies is what supports the state-switch reading.
