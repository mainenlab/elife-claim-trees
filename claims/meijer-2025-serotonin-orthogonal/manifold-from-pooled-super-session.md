The methodological claim is load-bearing for the orthogonality result and worth flagging explicitly because it carries two non-obvious assumptions. First, the pooled super-session approach treats the population manifold as a property of the collective recording, not of any individual mouse — the orthogonality result is therefore an existence claim about a brain-wide population structure, not a generalization across animals (the per-region per-animal version is in Fig. 5i but is also computed within the same pooled framework). Second, the block-aware null is essential — a naive shuffle of trial labels would underestimate variance because of within-block autocorrelation in choice and 5-HT condition; the paper's care about this is what gives the orthogonality test its bite. The 3-D PCA projection captures whatever variance is in the top three components but discards higher-dimensional structure; whether orthogonality holds in higher dimensions is not tested.
