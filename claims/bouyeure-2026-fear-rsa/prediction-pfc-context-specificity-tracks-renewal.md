This is the individual-differences half of the context-specificity hypothesis: a between-subject correlation that links a representational measure during reversal to a behavioral/representational measure at test. It is statistically the weakest of the predictions: with n=24, a brain-behavior correlation has limited power to detect anything but large effects, and the analysis is acknowledged by the paper as exploratory rather than preregistered. The prediction is tested by `pfc-context-specificity-predicts-renewal` and elaborated by the regional-dissociation finding (`context-specificity-predicts-acquisition-reinstatement-regional-dissociation`) and the dmPFC-reversal-trace finding (`context-specificity-predicts-reversal-reinstatement-dmpfc`).
