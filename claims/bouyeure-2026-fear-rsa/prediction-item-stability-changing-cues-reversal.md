This prediction is the item-specificity half of the dual-strategy account. It is anatomically displaced from the generalization prediction: whereas generalization targets the fear network (dACC, SFG, caudate, insula), item-specificity is predicted to show up in a different anatomical territory — precuneus, IFG, and broader PFC — regions associated in prior work with episodic detail and stimulus-identity coding. The double dissociation (different cue contrasts, different regions) is what gives the dual-strategy hypothesis its falsifiability: a single-strategy account would predict either generalization or item-specificity, not both in different regions.
