This prediction operationalizes the task-relevance half of the context-specificity hypothesis. During acquisition, every cue's threat value is fixed across contexts, so context-distinguishability provides no behavioral leverage. During reversal, the same physical cues acquire new meanings depending on context, so encoding contexts as separable representational entities becomes diagnostic. The prediction is therefore a phase-by-region interaction: PFC (specifically dmPFC and lateral PFC) should show elevated context specificity in reversal vs acquisition, while early sensory regions should not.
