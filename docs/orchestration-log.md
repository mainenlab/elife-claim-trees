## Paper 6 — headley-2026-inhibitory-rhythms

Biophysically detailed single-cell computational model of a layer 5 pyramidal neuron showing that beta rhythms (~20 Hz) at distal dendrites and gamma rhythms (40–80 Hz) at perisomatic locations gate distinct computational processes: distal-beta gates Ca²⁺/NMDA spike occurrence and thereby controls top-down (apical) input transmission, while perisomatic-gamma modulates AP threshold and controls proximal/basal input transmission. 18 claims extracted: 2 assessment (model scope, parameterization), 13 result claims (spike coupling timings, firing rate suppression quantitative effects, frequency sweep optima, bidirectional control, burst onset timing, input gating experiments), 1 null/boundary (E/I lag sensitivity), 2 synthesis (PV/SST interneuron correspondence). All result claims are `unverified` with clear reproduction paths via Dryad pre-computed data and GitHub notebooks. Assessment claims verified by README and code inspection. The GitHub README was critical — it provided an exact mapping from notebook to figure that allowed confident claim-to-panel assignment without the full paper text.

## Paper 4 — scheller-2026-self-prioritization

Psychophysics + hierarchical Bayesian TVA paper showing that arbitrary self-associations enhance early attentional selection automatically at the perceptual feature level, with a counterintuitive finding: when social identity decoding is required (social decision condition), there is no self-advantage and possibly an other-advantage. 15 claims extracted from the full paper text (PDF downloaded and extracted with pdftotext — this was the only paper for which the full quantitative results were available verbatim). Key claims include: the model comparison result (condition-specific capacity wins, Δloo=14.2), the self-advantage in perceptual decision (1.5 Hz, HDI: −0.16 to 3.2 Hz), the null result in social decision, the 6 Hz perceptual salience advantage, the additive interaction for other-associated stimuli, and the sub-additive interaction for self-associated stimuli. The inversion (no self-advantage in the social decision condition) was the most theoretically important finding and is captured as a first-class claim. All 15 claims are `unverified` — OSF data and Stan/R analysis notebooks accessible but not executed.

## Cross-corpus patterns

**Data accessibility:** All 10 papers satisfy eLife's data availability requirement at the level of the statement, but the actual accessibility varies enormously. Papers 4, 6, 9 (easy) are fully self-contained — data in the repo or on lightweight deposits, code runs in standard environments, pre-computed arrays cover the expensive steps. Papers 2, 5, 10 (medium) require either MATLAB/SPM12, heavy fMRI compute, or large OpenNeuro downloads — but pre-computed group-level results are available in repos or on NeuroVault, enabling figure reproduction without full re-analysis. Papers 3, 7 (hard) have primary claims behind wet-lab or proprietary model barriers that no amount of code-sharing can overcome. Paper 8 (hard) is an atlas paper where "reproduction" is structurally different (inspection, not re-execution) and data volumes are extreme. Paper 1 (medium) requires GPU infrastructure for inference.

**Verification landscape:** Across 81 total claim files written, approximately 15 are `verified` (by code or preregistration inspection), ~50 are `unverified` or `unverified:compute-infeasible` (path clear, not yet executed), ~15 are `unverified:no-data` (wet lab or imaging-scale barriers), and 1 is `unverified:no-code`. No claims are `failed:mismatch` — none were actually executed to the point of comparison. The easy papers (6, 4, 9) have the highest density of claims with clear reproduction paths; the hard papers (3, 7, 8) have the lowest.

**Method evolution:** The method changed in three substantive ways during this corpus run: (1) assessment claims are now explicitly classified as `verified` when confirmed by code/preregistration inspection; (2) the full status vocabulary (including `unverified:code-error` and `unverified:compute-infeasible`) is now consistently applied; (3) observational anatomical claims are recognized as a distinct type for which verification is atlas inspection. The method for Step 1 was also updated: always attempt PDF download first; eLife HTML is too truncated for full text extraction.

**Highest-priority reproduction targets:** (1) Paper 9 code-error claims (3 claims, one-line matplotlib fix each); (2) Paper 6 notebook-based claims (data in repo, conda env provided); (3) Paper 4 behavioral claims (lightweight OSF data, Stan/R); (4) Paper 10 decoding claims (standard Python pipeline, preregistered); (5) Paper 2 behavioral claims (MATLAB + .mat files in repo).
